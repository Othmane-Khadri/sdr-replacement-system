# Company Disqualifier Patterns

Use these patterns to disqualify companies during Gate 1 (headline) and Gate 5 (full qualification).

## Big Corporations (500+ employees)

Disqualify if the company matches (customize for your ICP):
Google, Alphabet, Microsoft, Amazon, AWS, Meta, Facebook, Apple, Salesforce, Oracle, SAP, Adobe, IBM, HubSpot, Cisco, Intel, Nvidia, Tesla, Netflix, Uber, Airbnb, Stripe, LinkedIn, Shopify, Zoom, Slack, Atlassian, ServiceNow, Snowflake, Databricks, Palantir, Twilio, Workday, DocuSign, Zendesk, Okta, CrowdStrike, Accenture, Deloitte, McKinsey, BCG, PwC, EY, KPMG, Capgemini, Infosys, Wipro, TCS, Cognizant, JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs, PayPal, Visa, Mastercard

## Agency Patterns

Disqualify if company name contains (case-insensitive):
- "Agency" (marketing, digital, creative, PR, SEO, branding, web design, development, recruitment, media, advertising)
- "Creative Studio" (unless clearly a software product)
- "Full-service" + agency keywords

## Lead Generation / Outbound Competitors

Disqualify — these are competitors, not customers:
- Lead generation companies
- Outbound agencies / SDR-as-a-service
- Sales outsourcing firms
- Demand generation agencies
- Appointment setting companies

Pattern matches: "lead gen", "outbound", "SDR as a service", "appointment setting", "demand gen", "sales outsourcing"

## Freelancers / Solo Consultants

Disqualify if:
- Company is "Self-Employed", "Freelance", "Independent Consultant"
- Company is the person's own name

**Exception:** Founder at a company with a real product name → do NOT disqualify.

## B2C Companies

Disqualify if primary revenue comes from consumers:
- Consumer apps (fitness, dating, social, gaming)
- D2C brands (fashion, beauty, food)
- Consumer marketplaces
- Retail / restaurants / hospitality

**Exception:** B2B2C is fine. Software FOR those industries is fine (e.g., restaurant tech SaaS).

## VC / PE / Finance

Disqualify:
- Venture capital firms, PE firms
- Accelerators/incubators (as employer)
- Hedge funds, family offices

**Exception:** Portfolio companies of VCs are fine.

## Non-Profit / Government / Academic

Disqualify:
- Non-profits, NGOs, foundations
- Government bodies
- Universities, research institutions

## Non-Software Industries

Disqualify:
- Construction, trucking, logistics (unless logistics SaaS)
- Law firms, accounting firms (unless legal tech / accounting tech)
- Real estate brokerages (unless proptech)
- Healthcare providers (unless health tech)
- Manufacturing (unless manufacturing SaaS)

Key distinction: "Acme Construction" = FAIL. "Procore" (construction SaaS) = PASS.

---

## Uncertainty Rule

When you can't determine what a company does:
- At Gate 1 (headline only): do NOT disqualify — proceed to enrichment
- At Gate 5 (enriched data): use web search to verify before disqualifying
- **When in doubt, keep the lead** — it's better to include a borderline case than miss a real prospect
